Draper: B2B Wholesale Pricing

Help center

Wholesale pricing, quotes, net payment terms and a B2B buyer portal — on the store you already run.

Overview

Draper layers wholesale pricing on top of your existing store. You don't migrate to a separate B2B storefront and you don't rebuild your catalog.

  • You tag your wholesale customers (or select them by name) and put them on a price list.
  • A tagged customer, once logged in, sees their own prices everywhere — product pages, collection grids, cart and checkout.
  • Everyone else sees your normal retail store. Guests and untagged customers never see wholesale prices (unless you hand out a discount code).

Under the hood the wholesale discount is applied by Shopify's own discount engine, so wholesale prices are correct in the cart, at checkout, and on the order — not just a number painted on the page.

One thing to know up front: Draper's pricing is discount-only. A price list makes things cheaper than retail. If you set a fixed wholesale price that is higher than retail, Draper leaves the retail price in place — it never marks an item up. See Fixed prices are discount-only.

New here? Start with Getting started, which takes you from install to a live wholesale price in about ten minutes.

Getting started

Step 1 — Install and pick a plan

  1. Install Draper from the Shopify App Store.
  2. On first open, choose the Draper Standard plan — $29/month with a 14-day free trial. You're not charged during the trial, and you can uninstall any time before it ends.
  3. After approving the plan you land on the Draper dashboard, which has a Get started guide mirroring the steps below.

Step 2 — Create your first price list

  1. Open Price lists → Create price list.
  2. Give it a name, e.g. Wholesale.
  3. Under Customer targeting, add the customer tag that identifies your wholesale buyers — e.g. wholesale.
  4. Under Default pricing, set a list-wide discount, e.g. 20% off.
  5. (Optional) add volume discounts, checkout terms, and more — you can always come back.
  6. Save. New price lists are Active by default.

Step 3 — Enable storefront pricing

Creating a list defines the rules; enabling pushes them to Shopify's discount engine so they apply at cart and checkout.

  • After saving, click Enable on the price-list index banner, or use the dashboard's Enable storefront pricing card.
  • The dashboard's Storefront health card turns green once pricing is live. You only do this once.

Step 4 — Show wholesale prices in your theme

The pricing engine works after Step 3. To also show buyers their price on the storefront, turn on Draper's theme pieces from the dashboard's Storefront features section — each card deep-links into your theme editor. Enable at least the product price block, collection prices, the quote drawer, and the cart blocks. Full detail in Storefront setup.

Step 5 — Tag a customer and see it work

  1. In Shopify admin, add your tag (e.g. wholesale) to a test customer.
  2. Log in to your storefront as that customer and open a product page — you should see the wholesale panel, a retail strikethrough, "Save 20%", and the volume ladder.
  3. Log out and confirm the same page shows plain retail.

Billing and the free trial

Draper uses Shopify Managed Pricing, billed on your normal Shopify invoice. The plan is Draper Standard, $29/month, with a 14-day free trial. Every feature is included — no add-ons or usage fees. Uninstalling cancels the subscription.

Storefront setup

Draper's pricing engine works as soon as you enable storefront pricing. Everything a buyer sees on the storefront comes from Draper's theme pieces, which you turn on in your theme editor.

App embeds vs. app blocks

  • App embeds run site-wide and are toggled in the theme editor's App embeds panel, then Save.
  • App blocks are placed on a specific template (product, cart, or a page) with Add block, then Save.

You don't need to remember which is which — on the dashboard, the Storefront features cards each open your theme editor at exactly the right spot. Click, then save the theme.

Quotes need the Quote drawer. Every "Add to quote" button opens the Quote drawer embed. If that embed is off, the buttons silently do nothing. Turn it on first if you use quotes at all.

Price lists

A price list is the heart of Draper. It answers two questions: who gets wholesale pricing, and what their pricing is. Edit a list on a single page (Price lists → open a list), organized in the order the rules resolve: Details, Default pricing, Customer targeting, Volume discounts, Order-value discounts, Collection pricing, Product overrides, Quantity rules, Checkout terms, Shipping.

Who gets wholesale pricing

Set this under Customer targeting. Two ways, usable together:

  • By tag — any logged-in customer with a matching tag (e.g. wholesale, tier-1) gets this list's pricing. The wholesale application auto-tags on approval.
  • Specific customers — search and select individual customers by name, for named accounts that should get pricing regardless of tags.

A customer must be logged in to be recognized. Guests never see wholesale pricing from a price list (only a discount code can price a guest).

Seeing customer names in the picker requires the "Protected customer fields (Name + Email)" permission. Targeting works without it, but the picker falls back to IDs. See Permissions & data.

Default pricing

The list-wide discount — e.g. 20% off retail — that applies to every product a targeted customer buys, unless a more specific rule overrides it.

Product overrides

Under Product overrides, set pricing for individual products and variants, more specific than the list default: percentage off, a fixed price, per-variant pricing (via "Add variants"), and a minimum quantity at which the override kicks in (a pricing threshold, not an enforced limit).

Fixed prices are discount-only

Draper applies wholesale pricing as a discount, so a fixed price only takes effect when it's below the item's retail price. A fixed price at or above retail is left as retail — Draper never marks an item up. The editor warns you inline when a fixed price is at/above retail so you can catch typos.

Volume discounts

Reward larger quantities with volume tiers — buy N or more and the discount deepens (e.g. 5+ → 25% off, 10+ → 30% off). Buyers see a live volume ladder on the product page and "add 2 more to reach the next price" nudges in the cart. The best qualifying tier applies automatically at checkout.

Order-value discounts

Reward larger orders: spend $X and get an extra Y% off the whole order, stacked on top of per-line pricing (e.g. spend $650 → 7% off, $1,200 → 12% off). The spend threshold is measured on the retail subtotal, and the cart's bar becomes an order-goals bar toward the next tier.

Collection pricing

Set one percentage off retail for every product in a collection. Draper expands the collection to its products when you save — a snapshot, so products added to the collection later aren't included until you re-save the list.

Quantity rules

Separate from pricing, Quantity rules enforce order limits per product or variant at checkout: a minimum, a maximum, or multiples of N (e.g. cases of 6). Buyers see hints with a one-tap "fix to a valid quantity" button; a variant rule beats a product rule. Adding to cart is never blocked — the check happens at checkout (and on the net-terms path, so buyers can't slip past through either route).

When a customer matches several lists

A customer resolves to exactly one price list. When more than one targets them:

  1. Specific-customer targeting beats tag targeting.
  2. Within the same kind of match, the lower priority number wins (1 beats 2; 0 is highest). Ties fall back to the oldest list.

The winning list's order minimum then gates that list only. Below it, the buyer gets retail — Draper does not fall through to a different, lower-priority list. So set minimums deliberately.

How the rules combine

For a given product, Draper uses the most specific rule available, in order: per-variant override → per-product override → collection pricing → best volume tier → list default. Then, on top of the resulting line prices, order-value discounts and free shipping apply if the order qualifies.

Active vs. draft & bulk CSV

Under Details, a list is Active or Draft; only active lists apply, and new lists are active by default. Set a list to Draft to pause it without deleting it. For large catalogs you can import/export per-variant pricing as CSV rather than editing rows by hand.

Checkout terms & shipping

These settings live at the bottom of the price-list editor (Checkout terms and Shipping) and control how a wholesale customer pays, their order minimum, tax, and shipping.

Net payment terms

Let approved customers buy now and pay later on terms — Net 15, 30, 45, or 60 days. On the cart page, an approved customer sees a Net terms button; clicking it places the order as a draft order marked payment pending on the chosen terms, and Shopify emails them the invoice. Turn on payment terms in Checkout terms and add the Net-terms button block to your Cart template.

The net-terms order runs through the same rules as a normal checkout — wholesale line pricing, order-value discounts, the order minimum, quantity rules, and free shipping all apply. It's a genuine parallel checkout, not a shortcut around your rules. Net terms is login-only; guests and code users can't use it.

Order minimum

  • Measured on the retail subtotal — before wholesale discounts, so a wholesale discount can't push a cart under its own minimum.
  • Adding to cart is never blocked. The minimum is enforced at checkout (and on net terms), with a clear message telling buyers how much more to add.
  • Wholesale customers see a live progress bar via the order-minimum cart block.

Tax-exempt automation

Draper can set Shopify's native tax-exempt flag on a customer, after which Shopify enforces $0 tax everywhere itself (checkout, net-terms drafts, reports).

  • Per-list toggle — in Checkout terms, "Mark these customers tax-exempt" marks the list's targeted customers on save.
  • On approval — the wholesale application approve row has a "Mark customer tax-exempt" checkbox, defaulting on when the application included a Tax ID.

It's a one-way switch by design: turning the toggle off stops marking new customers exempt but doesn't un-exempt existing ones (they may be exempt for other reasons). To reverse it, use the "Remove exemption from matched customers" button. A Tax ID field is evidence for your records — it never auto-exempts anyone on its own.

Wholesale shipping

Two independent mechanisms, both in the Shipping section:

  • Free shipping — for the list, optionally above a minimum order value (retail subtotal). Applies at checkout and on net-terms orders.
  • Rate customization — hide, rename, or promote shipping rates, matched by their displayed name (case-insensitive). Applies at checkout only — net-terms customers still get free shipping but not rate customization.

Discount codes

A Draper discount code unlocks one price list's wholesale pricing for anyone who enters it at checkout — including guests with no account and no tags. It's the way to give someone wholesale prices without tagging them first: a trade-show handout, a "first order" code for a prospect, a rep ordering for accounts.

How it works

  • You attach a code to one price list. Entering it at checkout applies that list's per-line wholesale pricing (default %, product/variant overrides, volume tiers, collections).
  • It works for guests and logged-in non-members.
  • A code defers to an existing wholesale account: a logged-in customer already on an active list keeps their own automatic pricing (the code does nothing for them, and can't stack).

What a code does and doesn't grant

Granted by a codeNot granted by a code
Per-line wholesale pricing (default, overrides, volume tiers, collections) Quantity rules · Net payment terms · Free shipping / rate overrides

The granted list's order minimum is respected — below it the code is valid but not applicable (the same retail-subtotal gate as automatic pricing). Quantity rules, net terms, and shipping overrides are account-only.

Create a code

  1. Open the Discount codes page from the app navigation.
  2. Choose the price list the code should grant.
  3. Enter a code (e.g. TRADESHOW) and an optional usage limit.
  4. Save — the code is live at checkout.

Because a code carries a whole price list's rules, deleting and recreating is the way to change what a code grants.

Quotes & RFQ

Quotes let a buyer assemble a basket and request pricing instead of checking out. You review it, set prices, and send it back; once accepted you convert it to an order and send the invoice — all inside Shopify, no separate email or PDF tooling. Quotes work for logged-in customers and guests.

Turn it on first. Every quote entry point opens the Quote drawer embed. If it's off, the buttons silently do nothing — enable the Quote drawer embed before anything else.

How buyers build a quote

  • Product page — the "Add to quote" button (works for guests too).
  • Collection / search / home cards — a quote button on each product card.
  • Cart drawer — a "Request a quote" button.
  • Variant quantity matrix — a bulk pad on multi-variant products; set a quantity per variant and add them all at once.

The slide-out quote drawer is the buyer's basket. For logged-in wholesale customers it shows a live estimate — each line's wholesale price and savings, volume nudges, and progress toward the order minimum. Submitting sends you the request.

My quotes (buyer self-service)

Add the My quotes block to a page and logged-in customers get a quote history with status, expiry countdown, and totals. They can accept a sent quote, decline, or duplicate one (re-loading those items into the drawer, re-priced). A buyer can't accept an expired quote.

Managing quotes in the admin

Open Quotes in the app navigation. Each quote's detail page lets you review the request (lines, note, requested delivery date, contact details), set per-line pricing, set an optional expiry, and track status (requested → draft → sent → accepted → converted / declined) on a history timeline. You can also duplicate a quote.

Send and convert

  1. Send the quote — the buyer sees it in My quotes and can accept.
  2. Convert to an order — Draper creates a draft order with your per-line pricing and sends the buyer a native Shopify invoice.

Conversion carries your exact per-line pricing onto the draft, so the price the buyer accepted is the price they're invoiced — down to the cent. Quantity rules are re-checked at conversion, so a rule-violating quote can't become an unpayable invoice.

Catalog control

Two independent features control what non-wholesale shoppers can see. They can run together.

B2B catalog lock

Hide prices and the Add to cart button from logged-out or non-wholesale visitors until they sign in or are approved. Products stay visible and browsable; only pricing and buying are gated. Enable the B2B catalog lock app embed — it's site-wide, with nothing to configure per product.

Catalog visibility

Truly hide wholesale-only products and collections from non-wholesale shoppers. Hidden items are removed from collection grids and search, and their pages are gated on direct access. A viewer counts as "wholesale" if their tags match an active price list, so visibility stays in step with pricing.

  1. In the app admin, open Visibility and choose the wholesale-only products/collections. Hiding a collection also hides its member products everywhere.
  2. Enable the Catalog visibility app embed.

Shopify renders a listing page's "N results" count on its servers, so after hiding items Draper replaces the now-inaccurate count with a short note (wording is a setting). This is a storefront/JavaScript layer, not a hard server-side boundary — hidden items can still be reachable through channels Shopify controls (sitemap, Storefront API). For hard catalog separation you'd need Shopify Plus / native B2B.

Buyer tools

Self-serve tools for wholesale buyers — a place to sign up, log in, reorder, and order in bulk. Each is an app block you add to a page (see Storefront setup); a common pattern is one page per tool, all linked from the wholesale portal.

Wholesale portal

A branded wholesale landing page that adapts to the viewer: a login prompt for logged-out visitors, a personalized hub (pricing at a glance + quick-action cards) for approved members, and an apply prompt for signed-in non-members. It gates instantly on the customer's tag, so the right state shows without a flash.

Wholesale application

A signup form for prospective wholesale customers — name, email, phone, company, and optionally a Tax ID / VAT number. Submissions land in Applications; approving auto-tags the customer (putting them on any matching price list) and offers to mark them tax-exempt.

Quick order form

A fast bulk-ordering form: search by name or paste/upload SKUs (CSV supported), set quantities, and add everything at once. Prices update live as quantities change, including volume breaks and quantity-rule hints. A block setting chooses the mode: add to cart, add to quote, or both.

Smart reorder

One-tap reordering from recent orders, each re-priced at today's wholesale rates with a savings hook. Buyers can add a whole order back to the cart or to a quote.

Reorder reads order history, which requires the order-data permission. Without extended order access, Shopify only exposes the last 60 days, so a buyer whose last order is older sees an empty reorder hub — a Shopify limit, accepted for launch.

Shopping lists

Saved, reusable order templates. A buyer saves a cart as a named list (e.g. Monthly restock) and re-adds it anytime, re-priced at today's rates. Lists are per-customer and read product data (not order history), so there's no 60-day limit.

FAQ & troubleshooting

"Nothing shows up" — setup

I created a price list but wholesale customers still see retail prices

Three things must all be true: (1) storefront pricing is enabled — the Storefront health card should be green; (2) the list is Active, not Draft; (3) the customer is logged in and matched (tagged, or named in "Specific customers"). Guests and logged-out customers always see retail.

The wholesale price panel doesn't appear on product pages

The Wholesale price on product pages block must be added to your Product template and the theme saved, and the viewer must be a logged-in, matched wholesale customer. The panel is intentionally invisible to guests.

"Add to quote" does nothing

The Quote drawer embed isn't enabled. Every quote button opens that drawer; without it they silently do nothing. Turn it on in the App embeds panel and save.

Badges or quote buttons don't show on collection pages

Enable the matching embed (Wholesale prices on collection pages for badges, Add to quote on collection pages for buttons). Badges show only to logged-in wholesale customers; quote buttons show to everyone. On a heavily customized theme, product cards may use non-standard markup — contact support with your theme name.

The volume ladder is a static table, not interactive

The live ladder needs the theme to expose a quantity selector on the product page. Some themes don't (e.g. a bulk "Quick order list" product page) — there, Draper shows a static volume table and Add-to-quote uses quantity 1 (adjust in the drawer). This is by design.

The Net terms button or order-minimum bar is missing

For Net terms: the customer must be on a list with net terms enabled and logged in, and the Net-terms checkout button block must be on your Cart template. The order-minimum bar block also goes on the Cart template and renders on the /cart page, not a slide-out cart drawer.

Pricing behavior

A fixed wholesale price isn't applying

Pricing is discount-only — a fixed price only applies when it's below retail. A fixed price at or above retail is ignored (Draper never marks items up); the editor warns you inline. See Fixed prices are discount-only.

A customer matches two price lists — which one applies?

Exactly one wins: specific-customer targeting beats tag targeting, then the lower priority number wins, then the oldest list. See When a customer matches several lists.

A tagged customer still sees retail

Usually one of: they're not logged in; the winning list's order minimum isn't met (below it the buyer gets retail, with no fall-through to another list); or the list is Draft.

Is the order minimum the discounted or retail total?

The retail subtotal, before wholesale discounts — so a wholesale discount can't push a cart under its own minimum.

Compatibility

Does Draper work with my theme?

Yes — Draper is built to work on any Shopify theme, not just Dawn. Its storefront pieces use scoped styling and don't depend on theme-specific code. On very customized themes an occasional piece may need a tweak; email support with your theme name.

Do I need Shopify Plus?

No. Draper works on any paid Shopify plan and doesn't require migrating to Shopify's B2B companies/catalogs.

Does it work alongside other discounts?

Draper's wholesale discount is applied by Shopify's discount engine and is set not to stack with other product discounts on the same line, so a customer gets their wholesale price rather than a doubled-up discount. If you run other discount apps, test the combination on a draft/test order.

Billing & data

How much does Draper cost? Is there a free trial?

$29/month with a 14-day free trial. Every feature is included — no add-ons or usage fees. Billing is handled by Shopify (Managed Pricing) on your normal invoice.

What happens if I uninstall?

Your subscription is cancelled and Draper's stored data for your shop is erased (via Shopify's GDPR/uninstall webhooks). Your products, customers, and orders in Shopify are untouched.

Why does Draper need customer data? Why does the picker show IDs?

To recognize who's wholesale (tags), let you target specific customers, and power Smart reorder (recent orders). The customer picker shows IDs until the Protected customer fields (Name + Email) permission is approved. Full detail: Permissions & data.

The Storefront health card

What is it, and what does "Repair" do?

The dashboard's Storefront health card runs read-only checks on the pricing engine (discount/validation/shipping pieces present, enabled, in sync). If it flags a problem, Repair automatically re-creates or re-syncs the underlying pieces. It's the first place to look if pricing suddenly stops applying. It can't check theme embeds (those live in your theme), so it honestly says "not checkable" for those.

Protected customer data

Shopify classifies some customer fields as protected. Draper uses three, each for a specific feature:

  • Customer tags — the core of the app; Draper resolves which price list, net terms, and visibility a shopper gets from their tags.
  • Customer name + email — shown in the admin customer search picker when you target specific customers. Read in-session and not stored; targeting still works without it (falls back to IDs).
  • Recent orders (last 60 days) — power Smart reorder. Read-only.

How your customers' data is handled

  • Reads happen server-side, tied either to the signed-in buyer on the storefront (through Shopify's signed app-proxy identity — a buyer can never request another buyer's data) or to your own admin session.
  • Draper stores only what a buyer submits — quote requests, wholesale applications, and shopping lists — plus Shopify IDs used for targeting.
  • No customer data goes to third parties. Error monitoring strips personal data and removes app-proxy query strings (which carry buyer identity) from reports.
  • Buyer-facing responses are sent with Cache-Control: no-store, so one buyer's data can't be shown to the next person on a shared device.

Data deletion (GDPR / uninstall)

Draper implements Shopify's mandatory privacy webhooks: customers/redact deletes a specific customer's stored quotes, applications, and shopping lists; shop/redact and app/uninstalled delete all of your shop's stored Draper data. Your Shopify products, customers, and orders are never touched by this — only Draper's own stored records.

Where data is hosted

Draper runs on Railway with a US-hosted Postgres database. Subprocessors are Shopify (platform), Railway (hosting), and Sentry (error monitoring, with PII stripped). Full detail is in the privacy policy.
